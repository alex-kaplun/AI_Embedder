The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It was named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower.

It is 330 meters tall and is one of the most recognizable structures in the world. Construction started in 1887 and was completed in 1889.

Paris is the capital city of France and is known for its art, gastronomy, and culture.
